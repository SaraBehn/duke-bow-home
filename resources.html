Investment Banking

Useful Guides
  • Mergers & Inquisitions 400 Investment Banking Interview Questions
  • Street of Walls
  • IBankingFAQ
  • How to Tell Your “Story”

Behavioral Interview Questions
  • Tell me about yourself.
  • Why investment banking?
  • Why do you want to work at this bank?
  • What feedback did you receive from your internship last summer?
  • What was your favorite part about your internship last summer? Least favorite part?
  • What are your strengths & weaknesses?
  • Where do you see yourself 5 years from now?
  • Tell me about a time you worked on a team.
  • Tell me about a time you experienced conflict on a team, how you responded, and what the outcome was.
  • Tell me about a time you were a leader.
  • What do you like to do in your free time?
  • What accomplishment are you most proud of?
  • What is your favorite class that you have taken?
  • Why are you studying (your major)?
  • What groups are you interested in?

Technical Interview Questions
  • What are the 3 valuation methods and which produces the highest and lowest value? (This question might also be asked for 4 valuation methods.)
  • What is the impact of $10 depreciation on the 3 financial statements?
  • Walk me through a DCF.
  • What is WACC?
  • Be able to identify which line items belong to which sections of the cash flow statement.
  • All else being equal, would a company prefer to buy another company using stock or cash?
  • What are the 3 largest companies in the world and what is their market cap?
  • Why would 2 companies have the same EBITDA multiple but drastically different P/E ratios?
  • Of Apple, Ford, and Snapchat, which would have an EBITDA multiple of 5, 15, and 30?
  • How would you value a local ice cream shop on the corner? What if it had no financials?

Other tips
  • Know the bank's CEO and stock price
  • Know what's on the front page of the WSJ
  • Follow a recent deal (you can find deal numbers in the fairness opinion, through investor presentations, or by browsing through news articles)
  • If possible, take John Caccavale & Emma Rasiel's Econ 256 class in the spring. It was so helpful for recruiting!

Capital Markets

Useful Guides
  • Mergers & Inquisitions 400 Investment Banking Interview Questions
  • Vault Guides

Behavioral Interview Questions
  • Tell me about yourself.
  • Walk me through your resume.
  • Why are you interested in this firm?
  • Why are you interest in capital markets? (the angle here is usually why cap-mkts vs. S&T or IBD)
  • Tell me about a time you've had to work in a team. What was your role on the team?
  • Tell me about a time you failed.
  • Strengths / weaknesses
  • If I were to contact your manager from last summer, what is some feedback he/she would give about you?
  • Tell me about a time where you experienced conflict on a team.
  • Say you have two MDs give you two different tasks you cannot feasibly complete within a given short time period. How do you prioritize these two tasks?
  • What do you like to do for fun?
  • What's the thing on your resume that you're proudest of?
  • What group within capital markets are you most interested in?

Technical Interview Questions
  • Tell me about a deal you're following right now.
  • Name three countries: one with a high interest rate, one with a middle interest rate, and one with a low interest rate
  • Would a company rather issue debt or equity to finance a project?
  • What do you think will happen with rates?
  • What is an IPO? What does an IPO model look like?
  • What are the factors you would look at in finding comparable companies in a comps analysis?
  • Where was the 10-yr treasury yield today?
  • What is the Fed Funds rate at?
  • What are you following in the markets?
  • Questions along this vein: what do you think will happen to the debt/equity markets if Trump wins the election?

Other tips
  • Technicals are markets-based, or conceptual bond/equity (i.e., general cap-structure) quetions
  • Have an understanding of the yield curve, bond math, and bond terminology
  • Technicals can be drawn from your previous finance experiences (if you have IBD on your resume, DCFs are fair game)
  • Have an opinion on some interesting deals (M&A, IPO, LBO), big news (elections, bankruptcies, etc.), and RATES!!
  • Understand broad macro news (what's going on in equity and debt markets, what's going on internationally)
  • Brainteasers, mental math asked a lot
  • Take ECON 256! Helped me a lot to understand a lot of the basics on valuation and markets concepts

Useful Guides

Behavioral Interview Questions
  • Tell me about a time you had to take risk and how did that pan out
  • Have you had to manage conflict in a group
  • Tell me about a time you failed
  • Walk me through your resume
  • Why S&T? Sales or trading?
  • What role do you play in a team?

  • What's a time you made a mistake and how did you resolve it?
  • Why x firm?

Technical Interview Questions
  • Who is the Fed Chair? Can you name someone else on the Federal Reserve?
  • What is the Fed funds rate at?
  • What are the three biggest drivers of the market right now?
  • Give me a trade idea based on what's happening in the markets now
  • What is the S&P/Dow/Nasdaq at?
  • What is EUR/USD, GBP/USD, USD/JPY etc at?
  • Would you rather be a sales person or a trader?
  • What are the mandates of the Federal Reserve?
  • Describe the yield curve
  • In what situation would the yield curve be inverted?
  • What are you following in the markets? (have at least 3 different areas you can discuss)
  • Stock pitch
  • Double digit multiplication, brain teasers
  • How would you invest $1 million?
  • What area/asset class would you like to work in?
  • are some macro trends you have been following?

Other tips
  • Take John Caccavale's class on Global Capital Markets
  • Self explanatory - but read the news!! WSJ, Bloomberg, Seeking Alpha, ZeroHedge
  • Keep a market journal to stay on top of the news, even just main headlines/articles and most importantly your view

Asset Management

Useful Guides
  • Read the WSJ in the weeks leading up to the interview
  • Investopedia is your friend

Behavioral Interview Questions
  • Name three areas of development.
  • How have you resolved conflict in a group setting?
  • What is your leadership style?
  • Speak to a time that you failed.
  • How do you prioritize homework and projects at school?
  • At which price is our stock trading currently?
  • Tell me a joke. Make sure it's funny.
  • Why isn't your GPA a 4.0?

Technical Interview Questions
  • Pitch me a stock as a buy. Now pitch the same stock as a sell.
  • Explain the utility of the Sharpe ratio in portfolio management.
  • How would you invest for yourself vs your parents vs the Duke endowment?
  • Is inflation or deflation a bigger threat over the next 5 years?
  • What are major events in the markets?
  • What differentiates market cap from total enterprise value?
  • Name two countries with negative interest rates. Why would a central bank issue negative interest rates?
  • Distinguish between strategic and tactical asset allocation.
  • Why would you run a regression analysis on a portfolio?
  • Explain trends in passive investing versus active investing. Which instruments/investment services align with each type of investing?
  • Speak to the importance of diversification in investing for institutions.

Other tips
  • If you don't know something, make that clear. Do not pretend to know more than you do.

Behavioral Interview Questions

  • Tell me about yourself.
  • Why are you interested in equity research? Why not investment banking or S&T?
  • Why this firm?
  • Strengths? Weaknesses?
  • Tell me about a time you failed
  • What's a time you made a mistake and how did you resolve it?
  • What do you like to do in your free time?
  • What accomplishment are you most proud of?
  • Why x major?

Technical Interview Questions
  • What are the main valuation methods?
  • What is the impact of $10 depreciation on the 3 financial statements?
  • Walk me through a DCF.
  • What is WACC?
  • Be able to identify which line items belong to which sections of the cash flow statement.
  • Why would 2 companies have the same EBITDA multiple but drastically different P/E ratios?
  • What are the 3 largest companies in the world and what is their market cap?
  • Of Apple, Ford, and Snapchat, which would have an EBITDA multiple of 5, 15, and 30?
  • What are you following in the markets? (have at least 3 different areas you can discuss)
  • Pitch me a stock.
  • Give me a trade idea based on what's happening in the markets now

Other tips
  • Create a few research reports in different industries. Create a 2-4 page write-up on a stock you like with an investment thesis, supporting facts, research and valuation models (comps and DCF and financial sattement projections). They should look pretty! Putting in the effort to create these write-ups shows an interest.
  • You will be asked for a stock pitch, which is why doing those write-ups helps. Practice a 1 minute stock pitch until you have it down. Have a few in mind, in various industries, because you don't have to pitch a stock to an ER that actually covers that stock
  • Same as S&T, keep a market journal to stay on top of the news, even just main headlines/articles and most importantly your view

Technical Interview Questions
  • market sizing
  • profit/loss
  • entering a new indsutry
  • developing/introducing a new product
  • determining pricing
  • growing market share
  • evaluating strategies
  • cost analysis and cost cutting techniques

Other tips
  • Come up with your own "why consulting" answer
  • Do lots of practice cases live with others in the recruiting process
  • Reach out to upper classmen who have gone through the process and can run a case with you
  • If you are going to be interviewing via Skype, Hireview etc. make sure to also practice cases this way since you have to do a bit more explaining since they can't see your paper etc.

Tech: non-technical roles

Useful Guides
  • There really aren't guides out there specific to non-tech roles at tech companies! Biggest advice is to do your research on the company: know their products and know where the company is trying to be 5 or 10 years from now.

Behavioral Interview Questions
  • Why are you interested in tech?
  • Is there something happening in the tech industry right now that you find particulary interesting?
  • Tell me about a time when you displayed leadership

Technical Interview Questions
  • Name something marketed well (or poorly)
  • How would you market X (to whom and through what channels)
  • How would you assess if X is contributing value to Y company?
  • What would you do to implement a new software package?
  • Develop a competitor to a well marketed product
  • The X unit is having trouble driving revenue, what kind of questions would you ask?
  • If asked to cancel a project you started, what aspects would you consider?
  • What has been your favorite class and why?
  • Tell me about a time you showed resilience.
  • Tell me about a time your project failed. Why did it fail and what did you do?
  • What's your favorite _____ product? How would you improve it?
  • What do you think will be the biggest obstacle for ______ in the next ten years?
  • Be prepared for "fun" brainteasers: "How many chairs are in the state of Kentucky?," etc You dont need to get the answers right! Just walk them through your thinking.

Other tips
  • For marketing, be familiar with the 4 P's and incorporate these into your answers: http://www.purelybranded.com/insights/the-four-ps-of-marketing/
